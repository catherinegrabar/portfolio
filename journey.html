Journey

Overview

Journey was a 6 week design-led sprint at Bing. The goal of the sprint was to create a vision for a differentiating end to end dynamic travel experience that can be built within 6-12 months. After the sprint was completed, we pitched it to the Bing Maps dev and PM teams to get resources to build the app. Journey is focused on the three stages of a trip: planning, traveling and reminiscing. Some of the experiences we focused on were creating personalized travel itineraries for the user, encouraging discovery during a trip and creating a beautiful journal of the trip for the user to look back on or share.

1. Planning a trip:

To help make trip planning less stressful, Journey uses Bing data to get you started with an itinerary based on your travel needs and preferences (budge, pace, interests, etc). From there, you can customize the itinerary by adding or removing some of the places. The goal of this stage is to help reduce the stress and effort needed to start planning a trip.

2. During your trip:

Once you're on your trip, Journey will help you explore and learn about your surroundings. Suggestions of attractions you might like show up on the map as you're walking around. If you're tired and need to take a coffee break, it's easy to search for places near you. When you're ready to continue on to your next attraction, the app will provide walking, transit or driving directions to help you get there.

3. After your trip:

After you come home, Journey puts together a beautiful travel journal filled with your photos, notes, sentiments and the places you visited. This creates an easy way to look back on a trip as well as share it with friends or others who are traveling to that location.

Process

The design sprint was split up into three phases. Phase one kicked off with 11 designers and in this first few weeks, we gathered inspiration, did competitive analysis, derived common elements we liked most from the moodboards and then organized them into themes. The three themes that we came out with were: My Trip (collections, itinerary, journal), Guide (city information) and Social (collaborating, sharing trip, sharing journal).
We continued exploring ideas by starting to sketch out some of the experiences. Out of that process came out a few key experiences: automagic itinerary creation, user seeding of the type of trip they're interested in, dynamic suggestions to change the itinerary on the go, dynamic map view and a beatiful visualization of the user's trip.

Outcome

This design sprint process had great interpersonal impact. It connected the maps designers with a few others who we usually don’t get towork with, it increased respect for the design process from our PMs and Devs and it inspired the maps team with the possibilities of what we could build. Although the app itself was not built, some of the concepts stuck around and influenced future maps projects.
